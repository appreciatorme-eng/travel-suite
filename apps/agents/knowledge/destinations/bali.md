# Bali, Indonesia - Destination Guide

## Overview
Bali is Indonesia's most famous island, known for its forested volcanic mountains, iconic rice paddies, beaches, and coral reefs. The island is a top destination for wellness retreats, surfing, cultural experiences, and romantic getaways.

## Best Time to Visit
- **Dry Season (April-October)**: Best weather, peak tourist season
- **Wet Season (November-March)**: Lower prices, occasional rain, lush landscapes
- **Sweet Spot**: May-June or September for good weather and fewer crowds

## Regions & Areas

### Ubud (Central Bali)
- Cultural heart of Bali
- Rice terraces, temples, art galleries
- Yoga and wellness centers
- Best for: Culture seekers, wellness travelers

### Seminyak
- Upscale beach resort area
- High-end restaurants and beach clubs
- Boutique shopping
- Best for: Luxury travelers, foodies

### Canggu
- Laid-back surf town
- Digital nomad hotspot
- Trendy cafes and co-working spaces
- Best for: Surfers, remote workers, young travelers

### Uluwatu
- Dramatic clifftop temples
- World-class surf breaks
- Beach clubs with sunset views
- Best for: Surfers, couples, sunset lovers

### Nusa Dua
- Calm beaches, family-friendly
- Large resort hotels
- Water sports
- Best for: Families, beach relaxation

## Must-See Attractions

### Temples
1. **Tanah Lot** - Iconic sea temple (sunset viewing)
2. **Uluwatu Temple** - Clifftop temple with Kecak dance
3. **Tirta Empul** - Holy water temple for purification
4. **Besakih** - "Mother Temple" on Mt. Agung

### Natural Attractions
1. **Tegallalang Rice Terraces** - Iconic stepped paddies
2. **Mount Batur** - Sunrise trekking (active volcano)
3. **Sekumpul Waterfall** - Most beautiful waterfall
4. **Nusa Penida** - Island with dramatic cliffs

### Experiences
1. **Traditional Balinese cooking class**
2. **Full moon ceremony at village temple**
3. **Sunrise yoga at rice terrace**
4. **Scuba diving at USS Liberty wreck**

## Practical Information

### Getting Around
- **Private driver**: Most convenient, $50-70/day
- **Scooter rental**: $5-8/day (international license required)
- **Grab/Gojek**: Ride-hailing apps (limited in some areas)

### Budget Guide (per day)
- **Budget**: $30-50 (hostels, warungs, scooter)
- **Mid-range**: $80-150 (nice hotels, restaurants, driver)
- **Luxury**: $300+ (villas, fine dining, private experiences)

### Local Tips
- Learn basic Bahasa: "Terima kasih" (thank you)
- Dress modestly at temples (sarongs provided)
- Negotiate prices at markets (start at 50%)
- Book drivers through your hotel or GoBuddy
- Carry cash for small vendors

### Health & Safety
- Drink bottled water only
- Use reef-safe sunscreen
- Watch for monkeys at temples (they steal!)
- Traffic can be chaotic - be careful on scooters
